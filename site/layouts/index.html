Question 5

Provide a link to documentation for a technical/developer-focused product, which you think are well done, and briefly explain why you think they are well done.

I’ve been using Nightwatch as the end-to-end testing solution for automation tests at my current role and have found the API Reference documentation on their website to be well done. It’s simple and easy to navigate, yet has everything that you would need available in each section. For example, if you click the “Expect” section, all terminologies that use “expect” appear in the sidebar so you can easily see each option, yet you can either click a specific terminology or scroll down through the page to see detailed information on all of the options that use “Expect.” To me, this is invaluable because there are certain terminologies that momentarily slip my mind, so the sidebar lists quickly trigger my memory. However, if I don’t know exactly how to word something, I can easily click on one of the options in the list and learn more about the exact wording.

Question 6

Why do you think SSL/HTTPS is important?

In my opinion, SSL/HTTPS is important for three main reasons:

  • Protecting the security and privacy of a website’s users. Ultimately, you do not want to hazard your customer’s personal data, and you cannot guarantee that your site is a safe one to visit without the security that SSL/HTTPS provides.
  • Ensuring trust between a user and that website. As someone who goes to many different websites daily, there’s something comforting to be said about the little green lock in the URL bar. When customers feel safe on your website, you will have a more loyal following due to the established trust that SSL/HTTPS provides.
  • Keeping that website as usable/findable as possible. Google has recently confirmed that they favor HTTPS sites, and over 40% of the sites found on the first page of Google are HTTPS. If you want your brand to be high up on the list of Google searches, simply SEO won’t cut it - you’ll want to also have HTTPS on your site.

Question 7

Explain, in a couple of paragraphs, what you think 2 major challenges around DNS configuration are for less-technical internet end-users.

For one, I think less technical users may not even know what DNS configuration is or understand why it is necessary. They may not realize that simply buying a domain name won’t automatically direct their website’s IP address to that domain name. In fact, they may not even realize that each website has its own custom IP address in and of itself!
Along with this, another challenge is that the steps for DNS configuration may be slightly complicated to understand and can vary greatly depending on the host website/where the domain name is registered, so it’s not easy to find one main source of information on how to do DNS configuration. A less technical user may not know how to look up the steps for DNS configuration and may get confused on how to even get started.
